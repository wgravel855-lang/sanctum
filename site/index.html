Why willpower keeps losing

You're not weak. The feed is built to win.

Endless content is one tab away, engineered to be frictionless and hard to close. Betting on in-the-moment willpower against an always-on system is a losing game — not a character flaw. Sanctum moves the decision to a calm moment and puts friction on reversing it.

The goal isn't to make relapse impossible. It's to make it slower than a craving.

How it works

Filtering below the browser.

No extension to uninstall, no per-browser setup. Sanctum works at the DNS and hosts layers, underneath every browser at once — and keeps running when the window is closed.

Every browser, no extension

Chrome, Edge, Firefox — and Incognito/Private windows — are all covered, because filtering happens below them.

SafeSearch, forced

Google, Bing, and DuckDuckGo are locked to SafeSearch; YouTube runs in Restricted Mode.

Keeps working

A background Windows service and a companion watchdog keep filtering on. Close the app or kill the process — it comes right back.

DoH-aware

It contains the DNS-over-HTTPS trick that quietly defeats most blockers, so encrypted lookups fall back to something Sanctum can filter.

Every part of Sanctum is visible in Task Manager and services.msc. It hides nothing — that's the point.

Locked sessions

We tell you the truth about removing it.

Start a locked session and the settings freeze: the block list can only grow, the timer can only be extended, and the password won't turn it off.

Here's the honest part most blockers won't say out loud — an administrator can still remove it by booting into Safe Mode. Sanctum doesn't pretend to be unremovable. The friction is real and bounded: enough to outlast a craving, not enough to trap you on your own machine.

Privacy

Nothing leaves your machine. Prove it.

No accounts, no servers, no analytics, no telemetry. Your block list, streak, and logs live only on your PC. Because Sanctum is open source, you don't have to take our word for it:

# any analytics or telemetry SDK?
> findstr /S /I /M "analytics telemetry sentry mixpanel" *.rs *.ts
# any HTTP client that could phone home?
> findstr /S /I /M "reqwest hyper ureq isahc" Cargo.toml
→ no matches. The only network Sanctum makes is the DNS it filters.

Free & sustainable

Free forever. Paywalling protection would be wrong.

Sanctum is released under the GPLv3 — copyleft is a deliberate choice for a recovery tool. If it helps you and you want to chip in, optional donations are welcome. But every feature that protects you will always be free.

Get started

Three steps.

Step 01

Get Sanctum

Download the release, or build it from source in a couple of commands.

cargo build --release
Step 02

Install the service

Run the installer once, elevated. It sets up the background protection and its watchdog.

sanctum-service install
Step 03

Start a block

Open the app, keep the starter list or add your own, and — when you're ready — start a locked session.
