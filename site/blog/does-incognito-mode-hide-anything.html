Does incognito mode actually hide anything?

From the makers of Sanctum

Incognito mode has a reputation it did not earn. A lot of people reach for it thinking it makes them invisible, or thinking it is a back door around a blocker. It is neither. It is worth understanding exactly what it does, because the gap between the reputation and the reality is where people fool themselves.

What it actually does

Private or incognito windows do one narrow thing well: they do not save anything on your device. When you close the window, the browsing history, cookies, and form data from that session are wiped from the machine. That is genuinely useful for shared computers, logging into a second account, or not leaving a trail in your own history.

What it does not do

Here is the part the name oversells. Incognito does not make you invisible to anyone outside your own browser:

  • Your network and DNS provider still see every site you look up, incognito or not.
  • Your employer or school, on their network, still see it.
  • The websites themselves still know you visited.
  • And crucially, anything filtering at the system level still applies.

Incognito is local privacy, not invisibility. It hides your session from the next person to sit at your desk. It does not hide it from the internet.

Why it will not get past a real blocker

This is the one that matters if you are trying to keep a promise to yourself. A lot of blocking tools are browser extensions, and many extensions are disabled in incognito by default - so yes, an extension-based blocker often does nothing in a private window. That is a real weakness, and it is exactly why people try incognito first.

But a blocker that works below the browser does not care what window you are in. When a site is blocked at the DNS layer, the lookup fails before any connection is made - and a private window makes the exact same DNS lookup as a normal one. Incognito, regular, a different browser entirely: all of them ask the operating system to resolve the address, and if that resolution is filtered, they all hit the same wall.

Sanctum filters at the DNS and HOSTS layers, underneath every browser at once - so Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and their private windows are all covered by the same block. There is no incognito loophole, because there is no browser setting involved. It is free, open source, and keeps working in the background.
